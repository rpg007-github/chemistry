Temperature Conversions

K = °C + 273.15

°C = (°F - 32) × 5/9

Water freezes: 0°C = 273.15 K = 32°F

Water boils: 100°C = 373.15 K = 212°F

Absolute zero: 0 K = -273.15°C

Significant Figures

  • All nonzero digits are significant
  • Zeros between nonzero digits are significant (101 = 3 sig figs)
  • Leading zeros are NOT significant (0.0045 = 2 sig figs)
  • Trailing zeros after a decimal ARE significant (2.50 = 3 sig figs)
  • Multiply/divide: answer has fewest sig figs
  • Add/subtract: answer has fewest decimal places

Classification of Matter

  • Pure substance: fixed composition. Either an element or a compound
  • Mixture: two+ substances physically combined. Homogeneous (uniform, like saltwater) or heterogeneous (non-uniform, like sand in water)
  • Physical change: no new substances (melting, boiling)
  • Chemical change: new substances formed (rusting, combustion)

Atomic Theory & Structure

Subatomic Particles

ParticleChargeMass (amu)Location
Proton+1~1Nucleus
Neutron0~1Nucleus
Electron-1~0 (1/1836)Electron cloud
  • Atomic number (Z) = protons = defines the element
  • Mass number (A) = protons + neutrons
  • Isotopes: same element, different neutrons

Ions

  • Cation (+): lost electrons. Metals form cations.
  • Anion (-): gained electrons. Nonmetals form anions.
  • Charge = protons - electrons
"Cation" has a "t" like a plus sign (+)

Atomic Mass & Isotopes

  • Atomic mass on periodic table = weighted average of all naturally occurring isotopes
  • Avg mass = Σ(mass × fractional abundance)
  • 1 amu = 1/12 the mass of carbon-12

Atomic Model History

  • Dalton: atoms are indivisible spheres
  • Thomson: "plum pudding" (discovered electron)
  • Rutherford: gold foil experiment, discovered small dense nucleus
  • Bohr: electrons in fixed energy levels
  • Quantum model: electrons in probability clouds (orbitals)

Electron Configuration & Periodicity

Quantum Numbers

  • n (principal): energy level (1, 2, 3...). Higher = farther, higher energy.
  • l (angular momentum): shape (0=s, 1=p, 2=d, 3=f)
  • ml (magnetic): orientation (-l to +l)
  • ms (spin): +½ or -½

Filling Rules

  • Aufbau: fill lowest energy first
  • Pauli exclusion: max 2 e⁻ per orbital, opposite spins
  • Hund's rule: fill orbitals of equal energy singly first, then pair
Order: 1s 2s 2p 3s 3p 4s 3d 4p 5s 4d 5p 6s 4f 5d 6p

Electron Configuration

  • Full notation: 1s² 2s² 2p⁶ 3s² 3p⁶ (for Ar)
  • Noble gas shorthand: [Ne] 3s² 3p⁵ (for Cl)
  • Valence electrons = outermost shell electrons. Determine chemical behavior.
  • Group 1A = 1 val e⁻, Group 2A = 2, ..., Group 7A = 7, Group 8A = 8

Chemical Bonding

Bond Types

  • Ionic: electron transfer (metal + nonmetal). EN difference > 1.7. Crystal lattice, high melting point, conducts when dissolved.
  • Covalent: electron sharing (nonmetal + nonmetal). Polar if EN diff 0.4-1.7; nonpolar if < 0.4.
  • Metallic: "sea of electrons." Conducts, malleable, ductile.

Lewis Structures

  • Count total valence electrons
  • Least electronegative atom in center (H always terminal)
  • Connect with single bonds, complete octets on outer atoms first
  • If central atom needs more, make double/triple bonds
  • Octet rule: atoms want 8 valence e⁻ (H wants 2)
  • Exceptions: B/Be (incomplete), period 3+ (expanded)

Polarity & IMFs

  • Polar molecule = polar bonds + asymmetric shape (dipoles don't cancel)
  • Symmetric molecules can be nonpolar despite polar bonds (CO₂, CH₄)

Intermolecular forces (weakest to strongest):

  • London dispersion: all molecules. Stronger with more electrons.
  • Dipole-dipole: polar molecules
  • Hydrogen bonding: H bonded to F, O, or N

Stronger IMFs = higher boiling point

Covalent Compounds

  • Nonmetal + nonmetal: use prefixes (mono, di, tri, tetra, penta, hexa)

  • CO₂ = carbon dioxide. N₂O₅ = dinitrogen pentoxide

Acids

  • Binary acids (no O): hydro___ic acid. HCl = hydrochloric acid
  • From -ate ion: ___ic acid. HNO₃ = nitric acid
  • From -ite ion: ___ous acid. HNO₂ = nitrous acid

Stoichiometry

The Mole

  • Avogadro's number: 6.022 × 10²³ particles/mol
  • 1 mol of any element = its atomic mass in grams (molar mass)
  • moles = mass (g) / molar mass (g/mol)
  • # particles = moles × 6.022 × 10²³

Balancing & Reaction Types

  • Conservation of Mass: atoms in = atoms out. Adjust coefficients only.
  • Combination: A + B → AB
  • Decomposition: AB → A + B
  • Single replacement: A + BC → AC + B
  • Double replacement: AB + CD → AD + CB
  • Combustion: fuel + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O

Stoichiometry Roadmap

grams A → moles A → moles B → grams B

  • Convert grams to moles (÷ molar mass)
  • Use mole ratio from balanced equation
  • Convert moles to grams (× molar mass)

Limiting Reagent & Yield

  • Limiting reagent: runs out first, determines product amount
  • Excess reagent: leftover
  • % yield = (actual / theoretical) × 100

Empirical & Molecular Formulas

  • Empirical: simplest whole-number ratio (CH₂O)
  • Molecular: actual atoms (C₆H₁₂O₆)
  • Molecular = empirical × n, where n = molar mass / empirical mass
  • % composition = (mass of element in 1 mol / molar mass) × 100

Gases

Gas Laws

LawRelationshipFormula
Boyle'sP ↔ V (const T,n)P₁V₁ = P₂V₂
Charles'sV ↔ T (const P,n)V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂
Gay-Lussac'sP ↔ T (const V,n)P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂
Avogadro'sV ↔ n (const T,P)V₁/n₁ = V₂/n₂
CombinedP, V, TP₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂

T must be in Kelvin for all gas law calculations!

Ideal Gas Law

PV = nRT

  • R = 0.0821 L·atm/mol·K
  • STP: 0°C (273.15 K), 1 atm
  • At STP: 1 mol gas = 22.4 L

Dalton's Law & Pressure

  • P_total = P₁ + P₂ + P₃ + ...
  • 1 atm = 760 mmHg = 760 torr = 101.325 kPa

Kinetic Molecular Theory

  • Gas particles in constant random motion
  • Volume of particles is negligible
  • No attractive forces between particles
  • Elastic collisions
  • KE proportional to temperature (K)
  • Lighter gases move faster at same T

Thermochemistry

Energy Basics

  • Exothermic: releases heat. ΔH < 0. Feels hot.
  • Endothermic: absorbs heat. ΔH > 0. Feels cold.
  • q_system = -q_surroundings

Heat Calculations

q = mcΔT

  • q = heat (J), m = mass (g), c = specific heat (J/g·°C), ΔT = T_final - T_initial
  • Specific heat of water: 4.184 J/g·°C

Hess's Law & Enthalpy

  • Hess's Law: ΔH is the same regardless of path
  • ΔH°rxn = ΣΔH°f(products) - ΣΔH°f(reactants)
  • ΔH°f of any element in its standard state = 0

Calorimetry

  • q_rxn = -q_water = -(mc ΔT) of water
  • Heat lost = heat gained (no heat loss to surroundings)

Solutions

Molarity & Dilution

M = moles solute / liters solution

M₁V₁ = M₂V₂ (dilution)

  • Moles of solute stay the same during dilution
  • moles = M × V(L)

Solubility & Electrolytes

  • "Like dissolves like": polar dissolves polar/ionic; nonpolar dissolves nonpolar
  • Strong electrolyte: dissociates completely (NaCl, HCl, NaOH)
  • Weak electrolyte: dissociates partially (CH₃COOH, NH₃)
  • Nonelectrolyte: no ions (sugar, ethanol)
